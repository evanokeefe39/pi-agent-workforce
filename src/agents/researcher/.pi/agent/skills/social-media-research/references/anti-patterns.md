# Anti-Pattern Evidence

## AP1: Ghost Followers
@theaikanteffect — 10,800 fans, 577 total hearts (0.05x ratio). Latest posts:
156, 201, 215 views from 10.8K base (1.5% reach). Content is generic AI
bootcamp promotion. Healthy accounts show 5-15x hearts/fans. Below 1x is a
reliable fraud signal.

## AP2: Volume Without Quality
@kirkstencell — 1,153 videos, 9,705 fans (8 fans/video). Compare @eggintech —
22 videos, 14,900 fans (677 fans/video). 85x more efficient. kirkstencell's
TOP posts (Type A) still hit 7.5% save rates, proving the format works — but
1,100+ diluting posts drown the signal.

## AP3: Viral But Not Saveable
@michellescomputer top posts: 14.2M views (0.03% saves), 8.3M views (0.02%),
2.1M views (0.10%). 22.6M combined views producing 8,354 saves. Compare
@learnwithseb: 941K views, 56,785 saves (6.0%). 7x fewer views, 7x more saves.

@eggintech 1.8M-view personal narrative: 203 saves (0.01%). Same account
produces 8% save rates with resource listicles.

## AP4: Post-Viral Niche Drift
@christieangelica: 972,600 views on AI tools video (7.4% saves), then 371
views on a quiz video. Immediate collapse, not gradual. The algorithm sees
low engagement from the AI-tools audience on the quiz content and stops
distributing.

@buildwithaug: 10,300 fans, 132,400 hearts (healthy), but latest 3 posts
get 305, 296, 412 views after shifting content tone.

## AP5: Generic AI Hype
@theaikanteffect: "Stop watching other people land $150K AI jobs" — no specific
tool, no specific skill, no specific resource. 156 views on 10.8K followers.
Compare @eggintech: "comment 'claude' and I'll DM the resources" — specific
tool, specific CTA, specific deliverable. 12,900 views, 8% save rate.

Quantified: posts naming specific tools produce 2.40% save rate vs 1.62% for
generic content. 49% lift.
