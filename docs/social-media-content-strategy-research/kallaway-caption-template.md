# Kallaway Caption Narrative Template
**Reverse-engineered from 12 Instagram posts (Jun 2026) | 438K followers**

---

## The Pattern

Analysed across all 12 posts. Every high-performing post (85K–1.12M likes) follows the same 7-beat script with minor variation. Lower-performing posts (< 50K likes) deviate from it.

---

## The 7-Beat Script

### Beat 1 — Opening Superlative Claim (1–2 sentences)
A single sentence that makes the subject sound impossible or uniquely important. Always positions the subject as the most/first/only/biggest of something.

**Formula:** `"This [subject] is the most [superlative] [category] in the world/ever/history."`
or: `"This [person/company] just [did something that shouldn't be possible]."`

**Examples from posts:**
- "This is the most insane tech company in the world...ASML."
- "This Russian engineer built a real-life Ironman suit in his garage."
- "These are futuristic contact lenses that let you see in the dark."
- "This company lets you order sunlight from an app."
- "Scientists just figured out how to reverse aging using AI."
- "This is the most insane creative platform I've ever used."

**What it does:** Creates a disbelief loop. Reader's brain wants to resolve "that can't be real" — the only way to resolve it is to keep reading.

---

### Beat 2 — One-Line Stakes (1 sentence)
Immediately answers "why should I care" with a scale or consequence statement.

**Formula:** `"[Every/All/Most] [relevant actor] [depends on / uses / is affected by] [subject]."`
or a direct implication statement.

**Examples:**
- "Every single computer chip on Earth is made using their machines."
- "It's the most movie accurate functional suit ever built."
- "They're like night vision goggles, without needing the goggles."
- "Wild."
- "And it turns out, the screen (biggest ever created), is the least impressive part."

**What it does:** Grounds the superlative in something real. Converts "wow" into "I understand why this matters."

---

### Beat 3 — The Pivot ("But here's the crazy/craziest part...")
The single most consistent element across all posts. Almost always the exact phrase "but here's the crazy part" or "but here's the craziest part."

**Formula:** `"But here's the crazy part, [escalation that makes it even more surprising]."`
or: `"And here's where it gets even crazier..."`

**Examples:**
- "But here's the craziest part, the way these machines work is completely mindblowing."
- "But here's the crazy part... he's been documenting the full 6-year journey on YouTube."
- "But here's the craziest part... these lenses let you see even when your eyes are completely closed."
- "But here's the craziest part... Reflect is starting to take customer orders starting in Q4 of 2025."
- "But here's the craziest part... They also used AI to ingest all 13,000 product descriptions."

**What it does:** Resets the hook mid-post. Reader who was about to scroll away gets pulled back. Creates a second reading loop.

---

### Beat 4 — The Explanation (3–8 sentences)
The actual content. Always structured as a linear narrative or numbered process. Never a list of facts — always a story with causality. Each sentence builds on the last.

**Formula:** Step-by-step explanation using "So", "Then", "From there", "Now", connective tissue.

**Structure patterns observed:**
- **Process chain:** "First X happens. Then Y. This creates Z. Z allows W."
- **Problem → solution → mechanism:** "The problem was X. They solved it by Y. Here's how Y works..."
- **Timeline narrative:** "In [year], scientists discovered X. In [year], Company took that and..."

**What it does:** Gives the reader the satisfaction of actually understanding something. This is what makes the content shareable — people share things that made them feel smart.

---

### Beat 5 — The Scale Proof (1–2 sentences)
A specific number, comparison, or analogy that makes the scale undeniable. Always concrete, never vague.

**Formula:** `"[Specific number/comparison that creates visual scale]"`

**Examples:**
- "The accuracy of the ASML machines is the equivalent of shooting a laser from the moon and hitting a penny on Earth."
- "50 year old cells and it successfully started repairing 30% of their cells in just 7 days."
- "He can do this with Cinema 4D, After Effects, Figma and more."
- "$7.6M in revenue…in 6 hours."
- "This AI stream did $7.6M in revenue... in 6 hours. That's more revenue than most of Luo's human streams."

**What it does:** Gives the reader a shareable fact. The analogy or number is what people quote when they send the video to a friend.

---

### Beat 6 — Wonder Close (1–2 sentences)
A brief moment of awe or reflection. Lightweight — never a big conclusion. Usually conversational.

**Formula:** `"[Light expression of wonder]. [Optional: forward implication]"`

**Examples:**
- "Shoutout science."
- "How insane is that?"
- "Science rules."
- "I think that, in a few years, complete body scans like this will be the new normal."
- "I think there is a massive opportunity here."

**What it does:** Signals the end of the explanation without a hard cut. Lets the emotional resonance land.

---

### Beat 7 — CTA Stack (2–4 lines)
Consistent format: follow CTA + optional engagement hook + hashtags.

**Formula:**
```
[Optional engagement hook — comment trigger]
Follow @[account] for more videos like this.
#ai #artificialintelligence #tech #technology #[topic-specific x3-4]
```

**Engagement hooks used (from posts):**
- "If you run a company and want to teach your team how to make videos like me, I put together a free guide. Comment 'viral' and I'll send it."
- "Comment 'storytelling' and I'll DM you free content newsletter."
- "Comment 'LUMA' for the PDF breaking down my full step-by-step workflow."
- "Comment 'Computer' and I'll send you both links."

**Hashtag pattern:** Always includes #ai + #artificialintelligence + #tech + #technology as base, then 3–6 topic-specific tags. Typically 8–10 total. No more.

## Adapted Template for Dev/Builder Account

The Kallaway script works for tech-wonder content. For a dev building in public, the same structure works but the subject shifts from "external thing that exists" to "thing I built or discovered."

```
I just [built/found/shipped] something [superlative].

[One-line stakes — what problem it solves or who it matters to]

But here's the [crazy/interesting] part...

[Pivot — the unexpected thing about it]

Here's exactly how it works/how I built it:

[Explanation — 4–6 sentences, process steps with connective tissue]

[Scale proof — specific metric: "in X minutes", "with Y prompts", "saving Z hours/week"]

[Honest close — what you'd do next or what surprised you]

[Optional: Comment "[WORD]" and I'll send you [resource]]
Follow @[account] for more.

#buildinpublic #vibecoding #aitools #[tool-specific] #[niche-specific]
```

## Key Differences from Standard Creator Advice

1. **No listicles.** Kallaway never writes "5 reasons why X" or "here's what you need to know." Always a narrative with causality.
2. **No generic hooks.** No "POV:", no "Have you ever wondered...", no rhetorical questions. Opens with a declarative claim.
3. **The pivot is the differentiator.** "But here's the crazy part" appears in nearly every post. It's not a formula tic — it genuinely works because it resets attention at the point where most readers would scroll away.
4. **Specificity over vagueness.** "50,000 droplets per second" not "lots of droplets." "Equivalent of shooting a laser from the moon to hit a penny on Earth" not "very precise." The specificity is what makes it shareable.
5. **Wonder, not opinion.** The close is never "this is bad/good/here's my take." It's always "isn't this wild." Keeps the content politically neutral and maximises shareability.
