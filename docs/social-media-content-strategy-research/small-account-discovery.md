## Content Format Analysis (from all 30 videos)

### What's working in this niche right now

**Format 1 — Counterintuitive safety/legal/risk angle**
@district_chronicles: "Everyone saying vibecoding is not safe — here's how you don't get sued." 28K views, 395 shares. Addresses the #1 objection people have about vibe coding in a practical, non-alarmist way.

**Format 2 — Complete reference guides ("everything you need to know")**
@buildwithfrancis: 60-second complete stack walkthrough. 2.5K views on 89 followers. High save/share because it's bookmarkable. People share it as a resource, not entertainment.

**Format 3 — Practitioner tool analysis ("here's what they saw in their data")**
@startscalr.com: 11K views on 1K followers. The hook is specifically about insider data/insight — "here's what most companies would ignore." Curiosity + credibility.

**Format 4 — Specific micro-example (the concrete story)**
@godmodepod: "A guy building a restaurant CRM just for the Swiss market." The specificity is the content. Not "niche SaaS works" — "here's a specific person doing a specific thing in a specific country."

**Format 5 — Opportunity sharing**
@shepherdttk: 267 shares on a hackathon post. Pure utility. Actionable, time-sensitive. Gets shares not because it's interesting but because people want to send it to someone who might use it.

### What's not working

- Generic AI tool lists without a specific use case or outcome
- Demo videos without a clear hook or surprising output
- Very short posts (<15 seconds) with minimal caption — not enough context to create a share impulse
- Sponsored/affiliate content (immediately visible, trust penalty)

## Gaps and Opportunities Confirmed

1. **No account in this scrape is doing "build diary" content** in the Marc Lou format — raw, daily updates on a specific product. This is the most underutilised format in the niche on TikTok.

2. **No account is doing screen-recording demos** of actually building something with AI tools (not talking about it, not explaining it — showing the screen as it happens). @rileybrown.ai does this at 637K followers. No one in the 1K–20K range is doing it with the same quality.

3. **The developer-practitioner voice is underrepresented.** Most content in the search results is either educational (explaining concepts) or motivational (you can do this). The "I tried this and here's what actually happened" honest practitioner voice is rare at under 20K followers.

4. **Opportunity posts (time-sensitive, actionable resources)** consistently get very high share rates regardless of follower count. A simple "here's a free resource/hackathon/tool launch you can use today" post can spike distribution significantly.
