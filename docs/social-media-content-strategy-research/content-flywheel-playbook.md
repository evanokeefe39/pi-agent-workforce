# Content Flywheel Playbook
## Solo Dev, Building in Public, Faceless AI/Tech Account
**Research-backed | June 2026 | Ready to execute**

---

## The One-Page Version

You record one screen-demo video per week showing something you built or a tool you used. That video becomes 6 other posts. You post 5–7 times per week across TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and X. You track share rate, not views. You recycle anything that hits 50K+ views. You don't use AI to write anything. You do this for 12 weeks before changing anything.

That's the system. Everything below is the detail.

# Section 1: The Strategic Position

## The gap that exists

After scraping and analysing content from creators at every scale in this niche, one position is consistently unoccupied at 100K+ followers:

**Developer practitioner, building in public, screen-first, honest.**

Not AI news. Not business owner tips. Not vibe coding for beginners. The specific position: "I'm a dev building real things with AI tools, here's my actual screen, here's what worked and what broke, here's the exact workflow."

Rowan Cheung (420K Instagram, 2M newsletter) — explains AI news to business people.
Kallaway (438K Instagram) — explains AI tools to business owners, science-wonder narrative format.
Riley Brown (637K TikTok) — now a product marketing channel for Vibecode.
Marc Lou (170K X) — builds in public but doesn't optimise for short-form.

None of them are you. The position is open.

## The three non-negotiables for 2026

**1. Never use AI to write scripts or captions.** Every creator at scale in this research said the same thing. Dan Koe (May 2026): "AI content is going straight to the bottom of the barrel." Riley Brown (Apr 2026): "Sounding like AI is suicide." Matt Gray (Mar 2026): "Content without proof is forgettable." Use AI for research compression and distribution logistics. Never for the content itself.

**2. Share rate is the only metric that matters early.** Views are algorithmic luck. Followers are a lagging indicator. Shares mean someone found it valuable enough to send to another person. Target: >0.3% shares/views. Small-account TikTok data shows accounts breaking out pre-10K are hitting 0.3–2.0% share rates. If you're below 0.1%, change the hook. Not the frequency.

**3. Platform first, then expand.** Pick TikTok as the primary growth platform and X as the community platform. Commit to both for 8 weeks minimum before adding anything else. The research shows every major dev creator built on one platform before expanding. Isenberg's threshold: 10K followers on one platform before splitting attention.

# Section 2: The Content System

## The anchor

**One screen-recording video per week. 12–20 minutes for YouTube. Filmed in Tella (chunk mode — restart individual chunks, not the whole video).**

Content falls into three buckets:

**Bucket 1 — Tool breakdown**
"I used [specific AI tool] to [do X that surprised me]. Here's the exact workflow."
Start with the output. Walk back through how. Include what didn't work.

**Bucket 2 — Build-in-public update**
"This week I built/shipped/broke [X]. Here's what happened."
Raw. No script. Failures are content. Marc Lou grew $0 → $20K MRR with this format alone.

**Bucket 3 — Practitioner news framing**
"[Company] just released [X]. I tested it. Here's what it actually does for someone building a product."
Not generic AI news. The developer angle. What you'd build with it. What the limitations are in practice.

**One rule across all three:** Outline only. No scripts. Speak from the outline. Your voice is the product.

## The repurposing chain

From one 12–20 min anchor, 6 derivatives within the same week:

| # | Format | Platform | How | Time |
|---|--------|----------|-----|------|
| 1 | Anchor video | YouTube | Record Tella → light edit in Descript | 90 min |
| 2 | Short clip A | TikTok | Most surprising 60–90 sec moment | 15 min |
| 3 | Short clip B | Instagram Reels | Second angle or follow-up moment (no watermark, re-export) | 10 min |
| 4 | Short clip C | YouTube Shorts | Searchable tutorial framing: "How to [X] with [tool]" | 15 min |
| 5 | X thread | X/Twitter | Core insight in 280 chars first, expand to 5–7 tweets | 20 min |
| 6 | Phone demo | TikTok/Reels | Quick screen recording of the output — 30 seconds, no editing | 10 min |
| 7 | LinkedIn post | LinkedIn | Business framing: what this means for someone building a product | 10 min |

**Total weekly production time: ~2.5–3 hours**

## The weekly schedule

**Sunday (content day, 2–2.5 hrs):**
- Record anchor in Tella
- Extract clips A and B
- Write X thread

**Monday:** Clip A → TikTok + Reels. X thread.

**Tuesday:** Phone demo or build update clip (30 min total, no editing). TikTok.

**Wednesday:** Clip B → TikTok natively. LinkedIn post.

**Thursday:** YouTube Shorts (searchable tutorial, recorded fresh, 20 min). Or second build update clip.

**Friday:** Full anchor → YouTube.

**Daily (30 min):** Reply to comments. Comment on 10 niche accounts. This is the relationship-building that converts viewers into followers — both Riley Brown and Justin Welsh credit daily engagement as critical in the first 6 months.

**Saturday (20 min):** Review analytics. Tag any post hitting 50K+ views as RECYCLE. Schedule reposts from secondary account via Typefully.

# Section 3: The Caption System

## The Kallaway narrative script

Reverse-engineered from 12 Instagram posts. The top performer: 1.12M likes (Iron Man suit). Average for video posts: 200K–400K likes. This structure is what drives shareability in the AI/tech niche.

**The 7 beats:**

**Beat 1 — Opening superlative (1 sentence)**
Make the subject sound impossible or uniquely important. Declarative, not a question.
`"This [subject] is the most [superlative] [category] in the world."`
`"This [person] just [did something that shouldn't be possible]."`

**Beat 2 — One-line stakes (1 sentence)**
Why it matters. Scale or consequence.
`"[Every/All] [relevant person] [depends on / uses / is affected by] this."`

**Beat 3 — The pivot (1 sentence)**
The most consistent element across all posts. Almost always this exact phrase.
`"But here's the [crazy/craziest] part..."`
`"And here's where it gets even crazier..."`

**Beat 4 — Explanation (4–7 sentences)**
A linear narrative with causality. Never a list. Use: "So", "Then", "From there", "Now", "This means". Each sentence builds on the last.

**Beat 5 — Scale proof (1 sentence)**
One specific number or analogy that makes it undeniable. This is what people quote when they share it.
`"That's the equivalent of [vivid analogy]."`
`"[Specific number] in [specific time]."`

**Beat 6 — Wonder close (1–2 sentences)**
Light. Never an opinion. Always awe or forward implication.
`"Shoutout science." / "How insane is that?" / "I think we see 10 of these by 2035."`

**Beat 7 — CTA stack**
```
[Optional: Comment "[WORD]" and I'll send you [resource]]
Follow @[account] for more.
#[4–7 hashtags]
```

## Adapted template for dev/builder account

```
I just [built/found/shipped] something [superlative].

[One-line stakes — what problem this solves or who it affects]

But here's the interesting part...

[Pivot — the unexpected thing about it]

Here's exactly how it works:

[Process — 4–6 sentences, each building on the last with connective tissue]

[Specific metric: "in X minutes / with Y prompts / saving Z hours/week"]

[Honest close — what surprised you or what you'd do next]

[Optional: Comment "[WORD]" and I'll send you [free resource]]
Follow @[account] for more.
#buildinpublic #vibecoding #aitools #[tool] #[niche]
```

## What does and doesn't work (from data)

**Works:**
- Opening superlatives with specific subjects ("This company" not "AI is changing")
- "But here's the crazy part..." as a mid-post reset
- Specific numbers and analogies (not "very precise" — "like shooting a laser from the moon to hit a penny on Earth")
- Linear process narratives, not lists
- 4–7 hashtags, not 0 and not 15+

**Doesn't work:**
- "POV:" openers
- Rhetorical questions as hooks
- Generic AI news without a specific practitioner angle
- Minimal captions on carousels relying on images alone
- Sponsored posts (visible 5–10x engagement penalty vs organic)

# Section 5: The 12-Week Launch Calendar

## Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4): Find what works

**Goal:** Identify one format with >0.3% share rate. Volume over polish.

**Week 1 — Establish what you're building**
Anchor: "Day 1 of building [X] in public. Here's what it is, why I'm building it, and the exact stack."
Production: Record anchor Sunday → extract clip A (show the thing) → write X thread (5 tweets, tweet 1 = the hook)
Post: Clip A Mon + Thu. Thread Mon. Build update phone clips Tue + Thu. Full anchor Fri YouTube.

**Week 2 — First tool breakdown**
Anchor: "I used [AI tool] to [do X]. Here's the exact workflow."
Key: Start the recording by showing the output first, then walk back through how.
New format to try: Extract clip B from the single most surprising step in the workflow.

**Week 3 — Practitioner news framing**
Anchor: "[Company] just released [X]. I tested it. Here's what it actually does if you're building with it."
New: Try the Kallaway narrative structure on one short clip this week. Track whether share rate differs.

**Week 4 — Build-in-public diary**
Anchor: "Week [X] building [product]: what I shipped, what broke, what I'd do differently."
Production: Cut together 3–5 moments from the week. Allow the failures — Marc Lou's failures outperformed his milestones.

**Week 4 review:** Which of the 3 formats got the highest share rate? That becomes your primary anchor for weeks 5–8.

---

## Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8): Build the repurposing chain

**Goal:** 5x/week consistently. Add LinkedIn. Track which posts hit 50K+ for future repost.

**Week 5:** Add one more short-form post/week (now 5x TikTok/Reels). Add LinkedIn 3x/week.

**Week 6:** Write one post/week using the Kallaway narrative script. Compare share rate to standard format. Keep whichever works better.

**Week 7:** Add 2 dedicated YouTube Shorts/week (separate from long-form clips). Format: "How to [specific thing] with [specific tool]" — searchable, compounds over time.

**Week 8 review — decision matrix:**

| Metric | Target | Action if missed |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Average TikTok views | >3,000/post | Change format, not frequency |
| Posts hitting >10K views | At least 2 | Change hook structure |
| Share rate on best post | >0.5% | Rewrite captions using Kallaway script |
| YouTube subscribers | 100–300 | Check if Shorts are optimised for search |
| X followers | 200–500 | Post more on X, engage more aggressively |

## Phase 3 (Weeks 9–12): Multi-account + recycling

**Goal:** Activate the repost play. Start recycling. Hit 1,000+ followers on primary platform.

**Week 9 — Create second X account**
Focused on a specific sub-niche (e.g., Claude Code specifically, or your product's exact niche).
Add to Typefully. Schedule your top 3 videos to repost from this account, 1 week apart.
Setup time: 1 hour. Ongoing: 5 min/week.

**Week 10 — Start the 6-month recycle calendar**
Tag every post hitting >10K views: RECYCLE-[original date].
Set reminder to repost 6 months later with refreshed caption.
Repost top 1–2 posts from weeks 1–4 this week. Track if performance is similar.

**Week 11 — Add lead magnet**
Pick the thing people ask about most in comments. Build a simple PDF or Notion doc of it.
Add "Comment [WORD] and I'll send you [resource]" to your next 3 posts.
Set up a comment auto-DM (ManyChat or equivalent).

**Week 12 — Full system audit**

Targets for 12 weeks:

| Platform | Conservative | Strong |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| TikTok followers | 1,000 | 5,000 |
| Instagram followers | 500 | 2,000 |
| YouTube subscribers | 300 | 1,000 |
| X (main) followers | 500 | 1,500 |
| X (secondary) followers | 200 | 800 |

**Month 4 decision:**
- On track → add email list (beehiiv or Substack, link from X bio)
- Below target → increase frequency AND change hook structure (both, not just one)
- One platform dominant → double down, reduce effort on others until 10K

# Section 7: AI Workflow for Research and Derivatives

Never use AI to write your content. Use it to compress research and generate building blocks you then write from yourself.

## The Dan Koe research compression workflow

1. Find a long YouTube video, research paper, or documentation on your topic
2. Paste the transcript into Gemini 2.5: "Summarise the key points, counterintuitive insights, and anything a solo developer building products would find immediately useful"
3. Use the summary as raw material for your own anchor video outline
4. Time saved: turns 2 hours of watching/reading into 15 minutes of notes

## The deep post generator (after writing your anchor)

Run your anchor content through this prompt to generate raw material for derivative posts:

```
Here is my content from this week: [paste transcript or outline]

Break this down into:
- 5 compelling post ideas (the most impactful ideas that could stand alone)
- 3 core paradoxes or counterintuitive truths
- Key quotes or moments I said that could anchor a tweet
- 3 transformation arcs (where → where)
- Core problems or pain points my audience has that this addresses
- 3 actionable steps someone could take today

Do not write finished posts. Give me the building blocks only.
```

Then write the posts yourself from those building blocks.

## The title generator prompt (for YouTube)

Build this once, update it as your best titles accumulate:

```
Here are my 10 best-performing YouTube titles: [list them]

Here is my anchor content for this week: [paste outline or transcript]

Generate 15 YouTube title ideas that follow the same psychological patterns as my best titles. Explain the pattern each one uses.
```

Pick the strongest, rewrite in your voice.

# Section 9: The Multi-Account Repost Play

**When to activate:** After you have at least one video hitting 50K+ views on X. Not before.

**The mechanics (Riley Brown's exact system):**

1. Create a second X account focused on a sub-niche (e.g., main = AI dev tools broadly; secondary = Claude Code specifically)
2. Add both to Typefully ($19/mo, supports unlimited accounts)
3. Every Sunday: identify your best video from the past week
4. Schedule it to post from the secondary account exactly 7 days later
5. Any video that goes viral continues to be reposted every week for the rest of the year
6. Eventually each account has its own narrow focus and its own algorithm profile

**Cost:** ~1 hour setup, 5 min/week ongoing via Typefully
**Result:** Riley Brown credits this with tripling his company's revenue in 2 months

**Scale-up path:**
- 2 accounts: solo manageable
- 3–5 accounts: need a simple system (Typefully queue + Sunday review)
- 7+ accounts (Riley's current): needs 2–3 people to manage properly

Start with 2. Let the content and revenue justify more.

# Section 10: What Not To Do

These are the failure modes visible across the research:

**Don't create fake build-in-public content.** If you're not actually building anything this week, post a tool breakdown or a practitioner news framing instead. The dev audience will clock inauthenticity immediately. Marc Lou's failures (DDoS attacks, wanting to quit, catastrophic iframe bugs) performed better than his success announcements. The audience is attracted to honest struggle, not performed hustle.

**Don't use AI to write scripts or captions.** Every creator at scale said this explicitly. Dan Koe built Eden specifically to help people NOT do this. Riley Brown said "sounding like AI is suicide in the long run." Your voice is the differentiator. Use AI as a thought partner, never as a ghostwriter.

**Don't chase views before understanding share rate.** Views are algorithmically distributed luck. A post with 500 views and 5 shares (1.0% share rate) is worth more than a post with 50,000 views and 25 shares (0.05% share rate). The first post's audience is sharing because it's genuinely useful. The second's algorithm boosted a mediocre piece.

**Don't expand platforms before 10K on one.** Greg Isenberg's rule: become the dominant voice in your niche on one platform before splitting attention. The research confirms every major creator did this. The multi-account play only works because Riley Brown built 500K on TikTok first.

**Don't batch-record content you don't care about.** Riley Brown: "The content where I'm really passionate in the thing is way better than content where I'm not passionate about it." Matt Gray: double down on the three formats you love, not the formats that seem to work for others. The constraint is: you have 2–3 hours/day and you're building something. Make content about what you're actually building and learning.

**Don't optimise thumbnails, titles, or distribution before week 5.** Until you know what content works, optimising the container is a waste of time. Riley Brown's advice: "Until you hit 50K subs, don't worry about optimising anything else." Build the content habit first.
