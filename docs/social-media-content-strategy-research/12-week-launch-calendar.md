# 12-Week Launch Calendar
**Solo dev, building in public, faceless AI/dev account**
**Platforms: TikTok (primary), Instagram Reels, YouTube, X**
**Budget: 2–3 hrs/day | No face required | No editing agency**

---

## How to Use This

Each week has: a theme, an anchor topic prompt, the exact production steps, time estimates, and the metric to track. The calendar assumes you're building something with AI tools — the content IS the build. If you're not building anything this week, use the "tool breakdown" fallback.

**Non-negotiables regardless of week:**
- Never use AI to write scripts. Outline only, then speak.
- Record in Tella (chunk mode) — restart individual chunks, not the whole video
- Post short-form with no watermark to each platform natively (don't cross-post from TikTok)
- Track one metric: share rate (shares ÷ views). Target >0.3%

---

## Phase 1: Establish the Anchor Format (Weeks 1–4)

**Goal:** Find one format that gets >0.3% share rate consistently. Post volume matters more than polish.

**Hashtag stack for every post:** #buildinpublic + #vibecoding + one tool-specific tag + one niche tag

---

### Week 1 — Launch

**Theme:** Introduce what you're building and why

**Anchor topic:** "Day 1 of building [X] in public. Here's what it is, why I'm building it, and the exact stack I'm using."

**Sunday (2 hrs):**
- [ ] Record anchor video in Tella — 10–15 min, screen recording of your project + talking through it. No script. Outline: (1) what is this thing, (2) why am I building it, (3) here's the stack, (4) here's what I'm doing first, (5) follow along.
- [ ] Write X thread (15 min): compress the same into 5 tweets. Tweet 1 = the hook claim. Tweets 2–4 = the three most interesting facts about what you're building. Tweet 5 = "follow along, posting updates daily."
- [ ] Extract 60-second clip: the moment you show what the thing looks like / does. This is clip A.

**Mon–Fri (30–45 min/day):**
- Mon: Post clip A to TikTok + Instagram Reels. Post X thread.
- Tue: Quick phone recording (30 min total): show one thing you built or broke today. 30–60 seconds, no editing. Post to TikTok.
- Wed: Post clip A natively to YouTube Shorts (no watermark — re-export from Tella). Write LinkedIn post: business framing of why you're building this.
- Thu: Record and post a "day 4 update" — what you shipped, what broke, 60 seconds.
- Fri: Post the full anchor video to YouTube.

**Metric to check Sunday:** Share rate on clip A. If <0.3%, note what the hook was and what you'd change.

---

### Week 2 — First tool breakdown

**Theme:** Show an AI tool that made something possible this week

**Anchor topic:** "I used [specific AI tool] to [do X that surprised me]. Here's the exact workflow."

**Sunday (2 hrs):**
- [ ] Record anchor: screen recording of the tool workflow, step by step. Start with the output (show what it made first), then walk back through how. 12–18 min.
- [ ] Outline before recording: (1) hook — show the output, (2) "here's exactly how I did this," (3) step-by-step demo, (4) what I'd do differently, (5) here's why this changes [thing].
- [ ] Extract clip A: the moment the output appears on screen — the "wow" reveal. 30–60 sec.
- [ ] Extract clip B: the single most surprising step in the workflow. 45–90 sec.
- [ ] Write X thread: the workflow compressed into 6 tweets. Lead with the output.

**Mon–Fri:**
- Mon: Clip A to TikTok + Reels. X thread.
- Tue: Build-in-public update (what you shipped/broke today). 60 sec phone recording.
- Wed: Clip B to TikTok. LinkedIn post: "here's how [tool] changes [something specific for builders]."
- Thu: Build-in-public update or a quick demo of something you tried that failed.
- Fri: Full anchor to YouTube.

---

### Week 3 — "What this means for builders" framing

**Theme:** React to a recent AI/dev release from a practitioner angle

**Anchor topic:** "[Company] just released [X]. Here's what it actually means if you're building a product with it — I tested it."

**Sunday (2 hrs):**
- [ ] Find the most interesting release from the past week. Spend 30 min actually using it, building a quick demo.
- [ ] Record anchor: open with the demo output, then "here's what this is, here's what I actually built with it, here's what worked and what didn't, here's what I'd use it for." 12–15 min.
- [ ] Extract clip A: the demo of you using the new tool. Lead with the most surprising output.
- [ ] Write X thread: lead with "I just tested [X] so you don't have to. Here's what actually works." Give 5 concrete findings.

**Mon–Fri:** Same rotation as week 2. Add: one "I tried to use [X] for [thing] and here's what happened" phone clip.

**Week 3 experiment:** Try the Kallaway narrative structure for one short clip. Opening superlative → "But here's the interesting part..." → explanation → scale proof → wonder close. Track whether share rate differs from your standard format.

---

### Week 4 — Build-in-public diary episode

**Theme:** Raw documentation of a full week of building

**Anchor topic:** "Week [X] building [product]: here's what I shipped, what broke, and what I'd do differently."

**Sunday (2 hrs):**
- [ ] Record anchor in "day diary" style — cut together 3–5 moments from the week. Each segment: what happened, what you tried, what the result was. Allow the failures. 10–15 min.
- [ ] This is the lowest-production format — literally just screen recordings you already captured during the week, narrated now.
- [ ] Write X thread: the most interesting thing that happened this week, framed as a lesson. Not a recap — pick one moment.

**Metric to check after week 4:** Which of the 3 formats got the highest share rate? That format becomes your primary anchor for weeks 5–8.

**Format options so far:**
- Tool breakdown ("I used X to do Y")
- Practitioner news framing ("what [release] means for builders")
- Build-in-public diary ("here's what I shipped/broke")

## Phase 2: Double Down and Build Repurposing Chain (Weeks 5–8)

**Goal:** Post 5x/week consistently. Add LinkedIn as a regular platform. Start tracking which posts hit 50K+ views for future multi-account repost.

---

### Week 5 — Increase frequency

**Changes from phase 1:**
- Add one more short-form post per week (now 5 posts/week on TikTok + Reels)
- Add consistent LinkedIn (3x/week — Mon/Wed/Fri)
- Start tagging viral posts for future repost

**Daily schedule:**
- Sun: Anchor recording (90 min) + 2 clips + X thread
- Mon: Clip A (TikTok + Reels) + X thread + LinkedIn (professional framing of anchor)
- Tue: Build update clip (phone, 30 min)
- Wed: Clip B (TikTok + Reels natively) + LinkedIn (lesson from the week so far)
- Thu: Tool tip or build update (TikTok)
- Fri: Full anchor → YouTube. LinkedIn (wrap-up or forward-looking post)

---

### Week 6 — Introduce the Kallaway narrative format

**Experiment:** Write one post per week in the Kallaway narrative structure. Can be the same content as your anchor, reformatted for the hook structure.

**Template (adapted for dev niche):**
```
I just [built/found/shipped] something [superlative].

[One-line stakes — what problem this solves]

But here's the interesting part...

[Pivot — the unexpected thing]

Here's exactly how it works:

[Process — 4–6 sentences, each building on the last]

[Specific metric: time saved, cost reduced, output produced]

[Honest close — what surprised you or what you'd do next]

Follow @[account] for more.
#buildinpublic #vibecoding #aitools #[tool] #[niche]
```

Post this as a TikTok on Thursday (separate from your other clips). Track share rate vs your standard format.

---

### Week 7 — Add YouTube Shorts properly

**Change:** Instead of cross-posting long-form clips as Shorts, record 2 dedicated 60-second Shorts per week specifically for YouTube. These are different from your TikToks — optimised for search, not feed.

**YouTube Shorts topic selection:**
- Format: "How to [do specific thing] with [specific tool]" — searchable
- Examples: "How to build a Stripe integration in 10 minutes with Claude Code" / "How to set up Supabase auth in one prompt"
- These compound over time via search — low views initially, steady long-term traffic

**Time:** Each Short takes ~20 min to record in Tella (one chunk, no editing).

**Decision matrix:**

- If YouTube Shorts are getting views faster than TikTok: shift primary platform to YouTube

## Phase 3: Multi-Account + Recycling System (Weeks 9–12)

**Goal:** Activate the multi-account repost play. Build a content recycling calendar. Hit 1,000+ followers on primary platform.

---

### Week 9 — Create second X account

**Action:** Create a second X account focused on a specific sub-niche of what you're building. Examples:
- Main account: AI dev tools broadly
- Secondary: Claude Code specifically / vibe coding / your specific product niche

**Setup (1 hour, Sunday):**
- Create account, write bio, pin your best existing post
- Add to Typefully (supports multiple accounts)
- Identify your top 3 performing videos from weeks 1–8
- Schedule each to post from the secondary account, 1 week apart, starting next Monday

**Cost:** $0. Time: 1 hour setup + 5 min/week maintenance via Typefully.

---

### Week 10 — Start the 6-month recycle calendar

**Action:** Tag every post that hit >10K views with "RECYCLE-[date]" in your notes. Set a reminder to repost 6 months from original post date.

**Recycling rules (from Justin Welsh's system):**
- Repost top performers at 6, 12, 18, 24 months
- Change the hook/caption but keep the core content
- On TikTok: natively re-upload (not repost) — same video, fresh post
- On X: quote-tweet with updated commentary, or repost from secondary account

**Week 10 action:** Pick your top 1–2 posts from weeks 1–4, repost them this week with a refreshed caption. Track whether they perform similarly to original.

---

### Week 11 — Introduce lead magnet (optional but recommended)

**The Kallaway play:** On your highest-performing content type, add a "comment [WORD] and I'll send you [resource]" CTA. The resource should be something you already have — your prompt library, your project setup checklist, your tool stack doc.

**Why this week:** By week 11 you should have enough content data to know what your audience wants. The lead magnet should be the thing people ask about most in comments.

**Setup:**
- Create a simple PDF or Notion doc of the resource (1–2 hours)
- Set up ManyChat or Manychat-equivalent to auto-DM when someone comments the trigger word
- Add the CTA to your next 3 posts

---

### Week 12 — Full system audit and month 4 planning

**Sunday review (45 min):**

**Followers by platform (12-week targets):**
- TikTok: 1,000–5,000
- Instagram: 500–2,000
- YouTube: 300–1,000 (Shorts-driven)
- X (main): 500–1,500
- X (secondary): 200–800

**Content system check:**
- [ ] Are you producing 5–7 posts/week consistently? (Y/N)
- [ ] Is the anchor recording taking <90 min? (Y/N)
- [ ] Do you have at least 3 posts tagged for recycling? (Y/N)
- [ ] Is your share rate improving week-over-week? (Y/N)
- [ ] Do you have one format that reliably outperforms others? (Y/N)

**Month 4 focus (choose one):**
- If followers are tracking toward targets: add email list (beehiiv/Substack, link from X bio)
- If followers are below targets: increase posting frequency and change hook structure
- If one platform is clearly working best: double down, reduce effort on others

## What Not to Do (Common Failures)

- **Don't batch-create fake content** if you don't have anything real to show. Inauthenticity is visible and the dev audience will clock it immediately.
- **Don't change platforms** because one feels slow. Commit to TikTok + X for 8 weeks minimum before reassessing.
- **Don't obsess over follower count** in weeks 1–4. Track share rate. 10 shares on 300 views (3.3%) beats 5 shares on 10,000 views (0.05%).
- **Don't wait for polish.** Marc Lou grew to $20K MRR with zero production value. Riley Brown's best early videos were raw screen recordings. The dev audience rewards authenticity over polish.
- **Don't use AI for scripts.** Every creator at scale said the same thing. Your voice is the differentiator.
